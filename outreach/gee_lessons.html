Introductory Google Earth Engine Exercises

I designed these exercises for a course I taught on applications of remote sensing. They are designed for students who are familiar with fundamentals of remote sensing -- e.g., a challenge in the first exercise asks students to make a false color composite.

These exercises are meant to be taught in a live coding format -- i.e., the instructor types out the code line by line on a projector, explaining key concepts along the way, while students code along on their own devices.

I am definitely soliciting feedback on these -- they are very much a first draft, so if you have any ideas or feedback, I want to hear it!

Earth Engine Coding Exercises

Exercise 1: Welcome to Google Earth Engine!

In this exercise, students learn fundamental skills in GEE, including loading in and visualizing ImageCollections and performing band math.

Exercise 2: Mapping Urban Green Space

In this exercise, students create a map of urban green space in a city of their choosing using spectral indices and thresholding. They also refine their visualization skills and try some new color palettes.

Student Starting Script Completed GEE Script

Exercise 3: Comparing and Contrasting Phenology

In this exercise, students choose two study sites to compare and contrast phenological dynamics. They learn to make charts, export data, and synthesize their findings into figures.

Student Starting Script Completed GEE Script

Exercise 4: Supervised Classification

In this exercise, students run a random forest algorithm to classify landcover types in Los Angeles.

Exercise 5: Terrain Analysis with LiDAR

In this exercise, students learn to process LiDAR data collected by the NEON AOP instrument, including calculating terrain properties (such as slope and aspect) and tree canopy height.
