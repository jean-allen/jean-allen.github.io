SARP Graduate Student Mentor Experience

NASA's Student Airborne Research Program (SARP)

The NASA Student Airborne Research Program (SARP) is a hands-on summer research program where undergraduates work with real airborne science datasets to plan, execute, and present independent research projects. As a graduate student mentor, I helped guide a total of 13 student interns through the process of turning messy, complex remote sensing data into meaningful science. I advised them on developing research questions, identifying analytical methods that fit those questions, and carrying out their analyses using a variety of tools, including ENVI, Python, and Google Earth Engine.

Working with SARP has been a huge highlight of my time in grad school. The two cohorts of students I mentored were incredibly passionate and motivated, and their projects were remarkable, resulting in 4 conference presentations. In the years after their internship, I've been very lucky to count several of them as colleagues as well as friends.
